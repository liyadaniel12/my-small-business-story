Building a Business from Scratch: My Story

I have always dreamed of becoming a businesswoman. When I was in grade eleven, I opened my very first online shop on Instagram, sharing products and connecting with customers in a way that felt exciting and full of possibility. Later on, I expanded my efforts to include a Telegram channel so I could reach even more people and create a stronger community around my small business. The idea for this venture first came to me during the COVID-19 pandemic shutdown, a time when everything felt uncertain, overwhelming, and out of balance. I wanted to find a way to stay productive and focused instead of feeling stuck. After I returned to school, I started by sourcing and selling second-hand clothes, which allowed me to build experience step by step. Over time, I slowly grew more confident year by year, learning how to present my products, communicate with customers, and trust my own abilities as an entrepreneur.

When my freshman year ended, my city, Bahir Dar, was under a state of emergency and there was no internet connection for several months. Because of this, I had to pause my business temporarily. I took online courses in digital marketing from Awaki to improve my skills and enrolled in a management degree at a private college called Alkan.

After that period ended and life slowly returned to normal, I joined the Information Systems department at Bahir Dar Institute of Technology to deepen my understanding of technology and business. Since then, I have continued running my online shop, expanding to platforms like Jiji and adding Facebook and Instagram promotions alongside my Telegram channel. Today, I am working hard to grow my business further, and I feel more confident and capable than ever. I believe this is just the beginning, and soon, I will add even more products and ideas to make my dream a reality.
